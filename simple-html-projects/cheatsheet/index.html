Fonts

There are two main types of fonts that we should be concerned about: Serif and San Serif

Serif

Serif fonts have small lines at the end of each letter. They are usually used for printed materials.

You make your design more serious, authoritative and older, so it is good if you are designing a letter head for a legal company or a magazine for architectural design.

font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif;

San Serif

San Serif fonts do not have small lines at the end of each letter. They are usually used for digital materials.

Also called Humanist, they are modern and clean. They don't add distractions to the eye, and do not try to be extravagant, they are simple yet progressive and are associated with adventure and modernity.

font-family: 'Arial', Helvetica, sans-serif;

Four Principles

Simplicity

Serving users what they need, when they need it, and in the most simple way possible.

Consistency

Users should be able to predict the behavior of the system. Also, the design should be simple and consistent, use 2 fonts at most, avoid mixing colors that give out opposite moods, etc.

Consider User Reading Patterns

Users read from left to right, top to bottom. Make sure that the most important information is placed in the top left corner.

There are two types of reading patterns.

  • F pattern
  • Z pattern

Responsive Design

Responsive design is a web design approach aimed at crafting sites to provide an optimal viewing experience—easy reading and navigation with a minimum of resizing, panning, and scrolling—across a wide range of devices (from desktop computer monitors to mobile phones).
